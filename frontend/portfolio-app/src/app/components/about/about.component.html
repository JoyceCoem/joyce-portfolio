About Me

I've always been drawn to how things work — and, more specifically, how they break. That curiosity for problem-solving is what pulled me toward tech in the first place, and cybersecurity felt like a natural extension of it: instead of just building systems, I wanted to understand how to protect them.

That's what led me to specialise in Security & Network Engineering rather than a more general IT path. I wanted to work on the defensive side — thinking like an attacker in order to build better defences, whether that's securing a network, hardening an application, or helping people recognise when something's not right, which is a big part of why phishing awareness became the focus of my final-year research.
